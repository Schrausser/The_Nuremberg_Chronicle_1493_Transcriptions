# Foliu[m]/Blat VIv

A *contemporary* English translation is presented from comparing the existing Latin, Early New High German (ENHG) and English (translation) texts for better comprehensibility. Especially since the ENHG text is difficult to understand compared to modern High German, as a *profound* knowledge of German (as a mother tongue) as well as already aknowledge of various *dialects* is required.

## The first age of the world

According to the Hebrews, the first age of the world, from *Adam* to the *Flood*, comprised 1656 years, while according to the **Septuagint**, **Isidore** and many others, it was 2242 years, which consequently became the standard time reckoning.

The *supreme goodness*[^1] wishes to impart this goodness to others, therefore it created a rational being who would understand the supreme goodness and thereby love it[^2], possess it [the supreme goodness] through this love and be blessed[^3].[^4]

God *now*[^5] created the first man by forming his body with the help of the angels from the clay of the earth in the field of *Damascus* and inspiring[^7] his *face* with the *breath of life*[^6], that is[^8], he created a soul[^9] which he united with the formed body. Thus, man was created in his natural attributes in the *image*[^10] of God and in his spiritual gifts[^11] in his *likeness*[^12]. Behold, the Lord bestows wonderful favor[^13] upon us.

>Cu[m] ergo mare[m] ad si[mi]litudine[m] sua[m] primum finxisset: tu[m] etia[m] feminam [con]figurauit adipsius hominis effigiem vt dou inter se p[er]mixti sex[us] p[ro]pagare sobolem possent: [et] omne[m] terra[m] multitudine opplere.[^14]

>FOrmatis a[n]i[m]antib[us] terre [et] volatilib[us] adduct[is] ad Ada[m] vt videret ea: cu[m] ada[m] no[n] i[n]ueniret[ur] adiutor si[mi]lis sibi: immisit d[omi]n[u]s sopore[m] in Ada[m] [et] tulis una[m] de costis ei[us]: reple[n]s carne[m] pro ea: [et] edificauit in muliere[m].
Qua[m] ada[m] vide[n]s: dixit h[oc] nunc os de ossib[us] meis h[oc] vocabit[ur] Issa q[uo]d lati[n]e m[u]l[ie]r interp[re]tat[ur]: q[uorum] de viro su[m]pta e[st].[^15]

After God created Adam, he led him to Paradise and there made *Eve* from the sleeping man's *rib*[^16], thus forming him a companion. He made her neither from the *head*[^17], so that she would not rule over the man, nor from the *foot* of the man, so that she would not be spurned, but from the *side*[^18], so that the bond of love would prove itself[^19].[^20]

This also shows that it is not the value[^21] of the place of origin, but one's own *goodness*[^22] that gives each individual[^23] person *moral character*[^24]. The man, created outside of Paradise, proves, although created in a *lesser* place[^25], to be *better for*[^26] Eve, who was created in Paradise.[^27]

God, the supreme creator of all things, thus created on the sixth day of the world, on March 25th[^28], after the animals of the earth, all the reptiles and birds, *Adam*, the first created[^29], first man, from a red clod of earth or clay[^30] in the field of Damascus[^31], *as the completion*[^32] *and possessor*[^33] of all creatures.
